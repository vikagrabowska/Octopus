# Octopus: Font/Typeface Design Testing Tool

Octopus is a font or typeface design testing tool for static and variable fonts that runs in your web browser.

**Try it at [m4rc1e.github.io/Octopus](https://m4rc1e.github.io/Octopus/)**

Initially created by Viktoriya Grabowska and Szymon Marciniak, it became a public libre software project in 2024 thanks to financial support from Google Fonts.
Special thanks to Eben Sorkin and Dave Crossland for making this happen.

Octopus was made to test fonts with large design spaces more efficiently.
It offers up to eight different views of a variable font, all at the same time.
It also allows you to choose your views on or in-between masters.

Octopus is useful as a QA tool, as looking in-between masters can catch inconsistencies in your letterforms or spacing, especially interpolation problems.

Octopus is also useful for comparing your design to other designs, your roman to your italic, and so on.

## Usage

Load fonts by clicking the "Font" button on any column, or by dragging and dropping font files (`.ttf`, `.otf`, `.woff`, `.woff2`) directly onto a column. Fonts stay in your browser — nothing is uploaded.

Pick a sample text from the dropdown, load your own `.txt` file, or type/paste directly into any column. A growing body of sample texts is available at [github.com/SorkinType/octo-text](https://github.com/SorkinType/octo-text).
